Phishing Website Detection Using Machine Learning

Phishing is a cybercrime that involves creating fraudulent websites to steal sensitive information such as passwords, credit card details, and personal data. As online transactions have increased, phishing attacks have evolved to become more complex and challenging to identify. Because phishing techniques are always changing, traditional approaches like heuristics and blacklists are frequently unsuccessful. The goal of this research is to create a machine learning-based system that can more precisely identify phishing websites. On the basis of characteristics including URL structure, website content, and visual signals, the system may learn to differentiate between phishing and legitimate websites by training algorithms on a sizable dataset of both types of websites. In order to develop a strong phishing detection system, the research investigates a number of machine learning approaches, such as supervised learning and unsupervised learning. The system achieved an impressive accuracy of 98.25% in detecting phishing websites. Additionally, we have published a research paper on this topic, sharing our findings and methodologies with the wider academic community.

Ultra Sonic Glasses for Blind

Ultra Sonic Glasses for the Blind are a wearable technology device designed to aid in the mobility of visually impaired individuals. These glasses are equipped with ultrasonic sensors that emit high-frequency sound waves, which bounce off objects in the user's path and return to the glasses. The device processes these signals and provides audio or vibration feedback to the user, alerting them to any obstacles in their path. This innovative solution enhances the independence and safety of visually impaired individuals by helping them navigate their surroundings more effectively.

Multi Multi-Renewable Energy Resource with Home Automation

This project aims to integrate multiple renewable energy sources, such as solar panels, wind turbines, and piezoelectric transducers, with a smart home automation system. By harnessing these diverse energy resources, the system ensures a sustainable and reliable power supply for household needs. The integration optimizes the use of renewable energy and reduces reliance on non-renewable sources. With the induction of piezoelectric transducers, power can be generated from human walking, adding an innovative dimension to sustainable energy generation.
